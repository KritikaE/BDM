## WEEK 1 — Micro & Macro Economics Basics

| Concept | Key Point |
|---------|-----------|
| **5 Economic Activities** | Production, Consumption, Exchange, Distribution, Investment |
| **Microeconomics** | Studies individual firms/households; focuses on prices, quantities |
| **Macroeconomics** | Aggregates — GDP, national income, sector-level data |
| **GDP** | Macro indicator; price links micro to macro |
| **Household** | Unit of consumption; makes decisions to maximize satisfaction |
| **Firm** | Unit of production; maximizes profit |
| **Utility** | Satisfaction from consuming goods — subjective, varies person to person |

**Survey Sources:** NSS (National Sample Survey), Consumer Pyramid Household Survey (CPHS — 200k+ households, thrice a year, panel data)

**Types of Data:**
- Time Series — data over time (e.g., GDP)
- Cross-sectional — data at one point in time (e.g., NSS)
- Panel Data — same household tracked over time
- Longitudinal — pooled time series + cross-sectional

**Survey Biases:** Time bias, Recall bias, Seasonal bias → NSS uses 1-day, 1-week, 1-month recall to minimize.

## WEEK 2 — Consumer Behaviour, Demand & Supply

### Utility Theories
| Theory | Approach | Measurement |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| Cardinal (TUC) | Numeric value (utils) | Quantitative — Marshall |
| Ordinal (TUO) | Ranking preferences | Qualitative — Hicks & Allen |

**Indifference Curve Properties:**
- Downward sloping (trade-off between goods)
- Convex to origin (diminishing MRS)
- Cannot intersect (transitivity of preferences)
- Higher IC = higher satisfaction

### Demand
- **Law of Demand:** Price ↑ → Demand ↓
- **Demand Curve:** Downward sloping
- **Shifts Right** (increase): income ↑ (normal good), substitute price ↑, complement price ↓, taste ↑, buyers ↑
- **Shifts Left** (decrease): opposite of above

### Supply & Equilibrium
- **Equilibrium:** QD = QS (market-clearing price)
- **Surplus:** QS > QD → price falls
- **Shortage:** QD > QS → price rises
- Market self-corrects through price mechanism

### Elasticity Summary
| Type | Formula | Key Insight |
|------|---------|-------------|
| PED | \|ΔQ/Q ÷ ΔP/P\| | Flat curve = elastic; steep = inelastic |
| YED | ΔQ/Q ÷ ΔY/Y | +ve = normal; −ve = inferior |
| XED | ΔQa/Qa ÷ ΔPb/Pb | +ve = substitutes; −ve = complements |
| PES | ΔQs/Qs ÷ ΔP/P | More elastic in long run |

## WEEK 2 — Production Costs

| Cost Type | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| Fixed Cost | Doesn't change with output (rent, salaries) |
| Variable Cost | Changes with output |
| Marginal Cost | Cost of producing one more unit |
| Opportunity Cost | Value of next best alternative forgone |
| Sunk Cost | Already incurred; irrelevant for future decisions |
| Explicit Cost | Paid to outside parties |
| Implicit Cost | Internal resource use |

**Short Run:** At least one input is fixed (usually capital)
**Long Run:** All inputs are variable

**Stages of Production:**
- Stage I: 0 to max AP (use more inputs)
- Stage II: max AP to MP=0 (optimal production zone)
- Stage III: MP < 0 (never produce here)

|----------|-------------|---------|
| Market Skimming | High price, low volume; for short lifecycle | PlayStation, Tech gadgets |

## WEEK 3 — Financial Ratios (Quick Reference)

### Liquidity
- **Current Ratio** = Current Assets / Current Liabilities → Ideal: **1.5:1**
- **Quick Ratio** = (Current Assets − Stock) / Current Liabilities → Ideal: **1:1**

### Profitability
- **GPM** = (Gross Profit / Revenue) × 100
- **NPM** = (Net Profit / Revenue) × 100
- **ROCE** = (Profit / Capital Employed) × 100

### Efficiency
- **Asset Turnover** = Sales / Assets
- **Stock Turnover** = COGS / Avg Stock (times/year)
- **Debtor Days** = (Debtors / Sales) × 365 → Lower is better

### Investment
- **EPS** = PAT / Shares
- **P/E Ratio** = Market Price / EPS
- **Dividend Yield** = (Dividend / Market Price) × 100
- **Gearing** = (Long Term Debt / Capital Employed) × 100

## WEEK 4 — Industries & Porter's Five Forces

### Porter's 5 Forces
1. **Rivalry** — More competition → less pricing power
2. **New Entrants** — Easy entry → existing firms lose share
3. **Supplier Power** — Few suppliers → you lose cost control
4. **Buyer Power** — Few buyers → you lose pricing power
5. **Substitutes** — Close substitutes → can't raise price much

### Concentration Indices
- **CR4** = Top 4 firms' combined market share
- **HHI** = Σ(market share²), Range 0–10,000
  - < 1000: competitive; 1000–1800: moderate; > 1800: concentrated

## WEEK 5 & 6 — E-Commerce & Inventory Analysis

### Key Terms
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| SKU | Stock Keeping Unit — unique product identifier |
| DC | Distribution Centre |
| MOQ | Minimum Order Quantity (supplier's requirement) |
| EOQ | Economic Order Quantity (optimal order size) |
| ROP | Reorder Point — trigger to order more |
| DOI/DOH | Days of Inventory / Days on Hand |
| Safety Stock | Buffer stock for emergencies |
| Stockout | Item out of stock — lost revenue |
| SOR | Sale or Return — pay only if sold (apparel) |
| Replenishment Frequency | How often stock is refilled |

### Two-Tier Distribution
- **Mother DC (H)** stocks in bulk → ships to **Child DCs (C, M)**
- Reduces replenishment frequency and MOQ cost
- Child DC daily topped up from mother

### Pareto Analysis
- **80% revenue from 20% SKUs** (Revenue usually follows Pareto)
- Volume may not strictly follow Pareto
- Revenue Pareto curve is steeper than Volume Pareto

### Portfolio Analysis (Scatter Plot Quadrants)
| | High Revenue | Low Revenue |
|---|---|---|
| **High Volume (Fast)** | Store near dock + secure | Store near dock |
| **Low Volume (Slow)** | Secure storage | Back of warehouse |

### ABC Analysis
| Category | % of SKUs | % of Value | Control |
|----------|-----------|------------|---------|
| A | 10–20% | 70–80% | Tight |
| B | 30% | 15% | Moderate |
| C | 50% | 5% | Loose |

## WEEK 8 — OEE, Scrap & Margins

### OEE Components
| Metric | Formula | What it measures |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| Availability | Run Time / Planned Time | Machine downtime |
| Performance | Actual Speed / Design Speed | Machine efficiency |
| Quality | Good Output / Total Output | Defect/scrap rate |
| **OEE** | **A × P × Q** | Overall productivity |

**World Class OEE ≈ 85%**

### Scrap
```text
Scrap = Hobbing Output − Broaching Input
Scrap Cost = Scrap Units × Blank Cost
```

### Profitability Layers
```text
Sales Revenue
− Direct Material Cost
− Direct Labour Cost
− Production Overhead
= GROSS PROFIT (Gross Margin)

Gross Margin
− G&A Overhead
= NET PROFIT (Net Margin)
```

### Inventory Management
- **Safety Stock** = Max Demand − Avg Demand
- **ROP** = Lead Time Demand + Safety Stock
- **EOQ** = √(2DS/H)
- Lead Time = Time from order to delivery

4. Company Career Portal

### Candidate Scoring (Normalization)
```text
Normalize each parameter to 0–1 range
Normalized = x / x_max  (or (x − x_min)/(x_max − x_min))
Score = Sum(normalized positives) − Sum(normalized negatives)
Use logical upper bound (e.g., divide ratings by 5, not by max)
```

### FinTech Evolution
Banks → Credit Cards (Visa/MC) → Payment Aggregators (PayPal, Razorpay) → BNPL (NBFCs)

### BNPL vs Credit Card
| | BNPL | Credit Card |
|---|---|---|
| Interest | None (short term) | Charged if unpaid |
| Check | Soft | Hard (CIBIL) |
| Limit | Low (~₹600 cap) | Higher |
| User profile | DC/NB users | High-income, loyal |

### Nudge Economics
- Design choice architecture to influence behavior
- Default options are powerful (people stick with defaults)
- Example: default payment method, cookie consent pre-checked

### RFM Analysis
| Metric | Meaning | Higher = ? |
|--------|---------|------------|
| Recency | Days since last purchase | Lower is better |
| Frequency | How often they buy | Better |
| Monetary | How much they spend | Better |

### BNPL User Insights
- Mobile users prefer BNPL over Web
- Electronics, Fashion, Pantry → high BNPL
- Travel → Credit Card preferred (high value + rewards)
- High engagement users → prefer Credit Card
- About-to-churn users → use BNPL predominantly

## WEEK 11 — A/B Testing & Credit Risk

### A/B Testing Rules
- Change **only ONE variable** at a time
- One group = Control (C), One group = Test (T)
- Check: sampling was representative (avg spends similar across groups)
- Check: T group shows statistically significant improvement over C

### Statistical Tests
| Scenario | Test to Use |
|----------|------------|
| Comparing means (normal dist.) | Welch's t-test |
| Comparing proportions (click rate) | Fisher's Exact Test |
| Poisson data (transactions per user) | E-test |
| Multinomial (products purchased) | Chi-squared |
| Unknown distribution | Mann-Whitney U |

### Credit Risk Mitigation
- Don't lend to risky customers
- Lend lower amounts to risky ones
- Charge higher interest for risky profiles
- Legal action for high-value defaults

### Reject Inferencing
- Build credit model on approved loans → biased
- Reject inference: infer whether rejected applicants would have been "good" or "bad" borrowers
- Improves scorecard accuracy

### Decline Reasons
| Reason | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Compliance | KYC not complete |
| Credit Risk | Predicted inability to repay |
| Eligibility | Age/other criteria not met |
| Fraud Risk | Suspicious intent |
| System Issue | Technical error |
